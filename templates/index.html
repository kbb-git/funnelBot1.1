What is Funneling?

Funneling is a questioning technique that helps sales reps discover customer needs by moving from broad to specific questions. It follows a structure of Thinking, Explore, Narrow/Confirm, and Sweeper questions.

Thinking Questions

Open-ended questions that trigger a new topic and provoke reflection. These questions usually start with "How" or "Why" and help establish the conversation direction.

Explore Questions

These questions broaden understanding of a topic by asking who, what, when, where, why, or how. They help gather more information about the customer's situation.

Narrow/Confirm Questions

These questions drill into specifics and confirm understanding. They typically use "if/do/is/are" formats and help clarify details about the customer's needs.

Sweeper Questions

Questions that surface anything missed or summarize the problem/next steps. Examples include "Is there anything else we should cover?" or summary statements.
